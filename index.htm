I am a dispersal biogeographer. Broadly, I take a demographic perspective to study the role of dispersal in shaping ecological and evolutionary patterns, using mathematical and Bayesian models. My current work explores population biology at the margins using theoretical ideas from network science quantitative genetics, coalescent theory,  population demography, and spread phenomenon. Click on the Science tab to see what I do.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Tim Keitt's Lab at the Department of Integrative Biology, University of Texas, Austin. I received my MSc in Ecology and Evolutionary Biology from Yale University. There, I worked on identifying the global determinants of the savanna-forest boundary. I received my undergraduate degree in Physics and Environmental Sciences from the Indian Institute of Science, where I did my senior thesis on regime shifts in complex dynamical systems with Vishwesha Guttal.
